Prison Mortality

Deaths by suicide is significantly higher among the incarcerated population than outside prison facilities.

BY ISABEL BONNET

Deaths by suicide is significantly higher among the incarcerated population than outside prison facilities.

Dante Taylor would spend up to $20 a day to call his mother, Darlene McDay, several times a day. But these phone calls stopped when he was placed in keeplock. People who are in keeplock spend 23 hours a day locked in their cell.

Three months later, McDay's phone finally rang. It was a representative from the Wende Correctional Facility in New York. He had news: Earlier that night, Taylor had taken his own life.

In 2019, New York correctional facilities had reached their highest rate of suicides in nearly a decade.

The day before his death, McDay learned that he had been sent to the hospital for what prison records called self-inflicted injuries.

When he was sent back to the Wende Correctional Facility, Taylor asked for mental counseling. But, instead, he was sent to solitary confinement, supposedly for his own protection. He died hours later, on Oct. 6, 2017.

Inside prison facilities, deaths by suicide have been on the rise nationwide.

The US has one of the highest rates of deaths by suicide inside prison facilities in the world.
